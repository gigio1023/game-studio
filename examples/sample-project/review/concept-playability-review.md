## Playability Questions

| Question | Answer | Review Concern |
| --- | --- | --- |
| What is the player fantasy? | Understand a strange building by watching it respond. | Good fit. |
| What is the first verb? | Shift one room rule. | Must be visually clear. |
| What changes because the player acts? | One path closes and another opens. | Needs strong before and after state. |
| What does the player learn in the first minute? | Their action changed the room and recovery is possible. | First minute may need non-text cue. |
| What can fail? | The player may take the blocked route or miss the opened route. | Failure must teach, not punish. |
| What is the smallest playable proof? | Three-room chain with shift, blocked path, recovery, exit. | Feasible. |
| What would make this concept worth rejecting? | The room change is not readable without explanation. | Clear rejection trigger. |

## Taste Survival

- quiet curiosity
- observation over reaction speed
- failure that teaches recovery

## Likely Cuts

- ambient story
- alternate art
- audio layers

## Rejection Triggers

- player cannot identify what changed
- the proof needs tutorial prose to explain the core rule
- recovery feels arbitrary
