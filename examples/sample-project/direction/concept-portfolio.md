# Concept Portfolio

## Recommended Concept

`Shifting Rooms`

## Why This One

It best matches the creator's taste for quiet curiosity, low reaction pressure,
and rules learned through visible consequence.

## Concept Slate

| Concept | Taste Fit | Target Player | Intended Emotion | Player Fantasy | Core Verb Clarity | Distinctive Bet | Main Risk | Proofability / First Proof | Production Risk | Reason To Reject |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Shifting Rooms | Strong fit for quiet curiosity and visible consequence. | Puzzle-curious players who prefer observation over reaction pressure. | Curious caution, then recognition. | Learn a building by changing one room rule at a time. | Shift is a single action with an immediate spatial result. | The same action makes the space both safer and riskier by closing one path and opening another. | The room shift may be visually unclear and feel arbitrary. | One room changes exits after one switch, creating a blocked path and recovery choice in a three-room chain. | Room-state readability could demand more art support than the solo proof budget allows. | Reject if the rule change is not readable in capture without explanation. |
| Echo Switch | Good fit for curiosity, weaker fit for low explanation. | Players who like planning around delayed consequences. | Clever anticipation. | Use recorded echoes of past actions to solve a room. | Record is understandable, but delayed playback may need framing. | The player cooperates with a past version of their own action. | The echo may feel like a puzzle script instead of a readable system. | One echo repeats a switch press while the player crosses a door. | Timing visualization and replay edge cases could expand implementation scope. | Reject if the echo mechanic needs heavy explanation. |
| Lantern Line | Good fit for visible consequence, weaker fit for low input friction. | Players who like drawing paths and seeing immediate material changes. | Gentle wonder. | Draw a light path that changes which objects are solid. | Draw is clear, but control feel must be tested early. | The player redraws what can be touched by placing light. | Input friction could dominate the first minute. | One line toggles two objects and opens a route. | Drawing controls and collision feedback may require more polish than the proof can afford. | Reject if input friction dominates the moment. |

## What It Gives Up

- no combat
- no inventory
- no broad map
- no story branch in the first proof

## Main Risk

The room shift may be visually unclear, making the player feel tricked instead
of curious.

## First Proof

Can the player understand that shifting the room changed the available path and
choose a recovery action within the first minute?
