# Direction Decision Record

## Decision Type

`selected concept`

## Decision

Select `Shifting Rooms` as the candidate concept for first playable proof.
Direction Lock is still pending.

## Authority Owner

Human creator.

## Context

The sample project starts from a taste profile that favors quiet curiosity, low
reaction pressure, and rules learned through visible consequence. The concept
slate compares three small, rejectable concepts before any production plan.

## Options Considered

| Option | Benefit | Cost | Reversal Cost |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Shifting Rooms | Best fit for observation-led learning and a one-verb proof. | The room-state change must be visually readable. | Low before Direction Lock; repeat or park after M1 if unreadable. |
| Echo Switch | Strong delayed-action puzzle hook. | Replay timing and explanation risk are high for a first proof. | Low if parked before implementation. |
| Lantern Line | Clear visual verb and immediate material change. | Drawing input could dominate the proof. | Medium if control feel work starts too early. |

## Chosen Option

`Shifting Rooms`

## Why

It gives the first proof one player action, one visible consequence, one
recoverable mistake, and one reviewable risk.

## Proof Basis

- `direction/concept-portfolio.md`
- `review/concept-playability-review.md`
- `production/first-prototype-contract.md`

## Rejected Or Parked Options

`Echo Switch` and `Lantern Line` are parked, not rejected.

## First Proof Target

Can the player understand that shifting the room changed the available path and
choose a recovery action within the first minute?

## Stage Change Requested

`Protocol Proof`

## Current Concept Status

Candidate direction accepted for proof. Direction Lock remains pending until the
first playable review reaches `READY`.

## Accepted Risk

The main accepted risk is first-minute readability: the player may understand
that something changed without noticing which route opened.

## Review Gate

Concept playability review, then first playable review.
