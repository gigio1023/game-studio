# Creative Brief

## Selected Concept

`Shifting Rooms`

## Player Promise

Learn a small changing space by making one clear action, seeing the consequence,
and using that consequence to choose the next route.

## Target Player

Players who like quiet puzzle spaces and observation-based learning without
combat or timed pressure.

## Player Fantasy

I can understand a strange building by watching how it responds to me.

## Core Verb

Shift one room rule.

## Core Pressure

The same shift that opens one path can block another.

## Distinctive Bet

One quiet action should make the room feel newly understandable: the player
learns by watching a path close, finding the path that opened, and choosing a
recovery route without tutorial prose.

## Main Risk

The first-minute read may be too weak. If the opened route does not receive
enough visual priority, the shift can feel arbitrary instead of learnable.

## Intended Emotion

Curious caution, then recognition.

## Creative Pillars

- Every rule change is visible.
- Failure teaches the next attempt.
- The first proof stays small enough to finish and review.

## Anti-Pillars

- No arbitrary puzzle locks.
- No combat pressure.
- No lore replacing interaction.

## First Playable Proof

One executable room chain with a shift, blocked path, recovery, and final exit.

## Proof Budget

- one week
- one player verb
- three rooms
- placeholder art
- one gameplay capture note
- one lean review

## What To Ignore For Now

- story delivery
- final art style
- save system
- broad content plan
