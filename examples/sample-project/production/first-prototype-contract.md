# First Prototype Contract

## Selected Concept

`Shifting Rooms`

## Prototype Question

Can the player understand that one shift changed the available path without
reading tutorial text?

## Player Moment To Prove

The player presses shift, sees the east door close and the north door open, then
chooses a recovery route.

## Direction Source

`taste-to-game`

## Proof Budget

| Constraint | Budget | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Calendar time | 2 days | Prototype before full first playable build. |
| People | 1 | Solo creator. |
| Tools | Existing generic 2D runtime | No new engine. |
| Mechanics | move, shift | No inventory. |
| Content | one room sketch | Paper or blockout is acceptable. |
| Review | concept owner plus QA | Lean review. |

## Minimum Playable Loop

```text
Player action -> rule response -> visible consequence -> next choice
```

## What To Build

- one blockout room
- one shift interaction
- one changed exit

## What To Ignore

- final art
- audio
- story text
- save system

## What To Capture

- screenshot or short capture showing before and after shift
- note on whether the changed exit is readable

## Review Roles

- Game Director
- QA Lead

## What Would Change The Direction

If the shift only makes sense with explanation, the concept should narrow toward
a more direct spatial change or park the room-shift idea.

## Stop Condition

Stop after the first shift can be observed and reviewed. Do not add new rooms
until this moment is readable.

## Decision Record

Proceed with first playable proof after concept playability review.
