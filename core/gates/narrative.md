# Narrative Gates

Narrative gates protect story as playable design work.

## GS-NARRATIVE-PROMISE

Question: Does the narrative promise support the game thesis and player role?

Review:

- The story promise changes what the player does, reads, risks, or decides.
- The player role in the story is clear.
- The emotional arc can be tested in play.
- Narrative anti-pillars reject generic lore or cinematic overreach.

## GS-SCENARIO-LEGIBILITY

Question: Can the player understand and act on the scenario?

Review:

- Each critical beat has a player verb.
- Player knowledge before and after the beat is explicit.
- Required clues, state changes, and failure paths are documented.
- Playtest evidence checks comprehension, motivation, and pacing.

## GS-CONTENT-SCOPE

Question: Is the narrative content scope production-ready?

Review:

- Word count, branch count, VO minutes, strings, and state flags are estimated.
- Branches have coverage and collapse rules.
- Localization, accessibility, and content QA needs are named.
- Canon authority and AI-assisted text boundaries are explicit.
