# Production Gates

Production gates decide whether the project can advance to broader work.

## GS-PROTOCOL-PROOF

Question: Has the project proven its central rule loop before broad production?

Review:

- The minimal loop exists.
- Success and failure are both observable.
- Evidence is produced.
- The player behavior being proven is named.
- The proof maps to the thesis and pillars.

## GS-PREPRODUCTION-EXIT

Question: Can the project enter production without open-ended discovery?

Review:

- The core loop has playable evidence.
- Production scope, cut candidates, dependencies, and capacity assumptions are named.
- Technical, content, QA, accessibility, localization, and release risks are assigned.
- The next milestone has quality criteria and a fallback proof.

## GS-PRESENTATION-VALUE

Question: Does the chosen presentation format earn its cost?

Review:

- 3D, 2D, text, or hybrid presentation produces gameplay value.
- Camera and input are playable.
- Visual and audio direction support the experience target.
- Moment-to-moment capture proves read, input, feedback, camera, visual hierarchy, audio priority, and recovery.
- Presentation is not hiding a missing core loop.

## GS-VERTICAL-SLICE

Question: Is this a true vertical slice?

Review:

- The slice is short and production-representative.
- Gameplay, content, UI, audio, visual quality, and technical stability align.
- External playtest evidence exists.
- Known accessibility, localization, content, and release risks are bounded.
- Known missing features are outside the slice promise.
