# Playtest Rubric

Use this for internal and external playtest reports.

## Observe

- What research question is being tested?
- Did the player understand the immediate goal?
- Did the player perform the intended action?
- Did the player notice feedback?
- Did the player experience the target emotion?
- Did the player recover from failure?
- Did the player hit an accessibility barrier?
- Did camera, input, UI, visual hierarchy, or audio block comprehension?
- Where did confusion become frustration?
- Where did the build promise something it could not deliver?

## Record

- Build identifier
- Scenario
- Player profile
- Session length
- Input device
- Task success or failure
- Key observations
- Inferences separated from observations
- Top three observed issues
- Top three successful moments
- Severity and proof strength
- Retest recommendation
- Internal evidence links
- Recommended gate verdict

## Triangulation

- One source: hypothesis, investigate only.
- Two source types: pattern, schedule or prioritize.
- Three source types: high-confidence finding, block or fix depending on
  severity.

Source types:

- Observation: player behavior, hesitation, body language, timing, repeated
  attempts, or stopping point.
- Metric: completion rate, time, count, retry, churn point, or error frequency.
- Interview: player words, separated from facilitator interpretation.

## Severity By Frequency

- Critical plus high frequency: fix before next playtest.
- Critical plus segment frequency: investigate root cause and likely fix.
- High plus high frequency: fix in next sprint.
- Medium or low: backlog unless tied to milestone proof.

## Discovery Use

- Before a playable build exists, walkthroughs, paper prototypes, and reference
  analysis are weak proof only.
- After the first playable proof exists, prefer observed behavior over opinion.
- Do not turn "I like this concept" into production approval without a prototype
  question and review verdict.
