# Direction Quality Rubric

Use this rubric before implementation starts or when a direction document changes.

## READY

- The thesis names a player role, core action, pressure, and consequence.
- Pillars can reject features, content, and presentation choices.
- Anti-pillars block generic genre drift.
- The target player and first-session experience are concrete.
- The presentation format earns its production cost.
- AI authority, if present, has a deterministic boundary.
- The plan names the first playable proof.
- The concept can pass a concept playability review.
- The first proof has a budget and rejection trigger.

## CONCERNS

- The thesis is playable but weakly differentiated.
- The target emotion exists, but proof is not yet defined.
- The first proof exists but needs a sharper review question.
- Pillars overlap but still guide some decisions.
- Presentation value is plausible but unproven.
- Risks are named but not assigned to a proof.

## NOT_READY

- The direction is mostly lore, theme, genre, or mood.
- The player action is unclear.
- The plan cannot reject a tempting wrong direction.
- The intended experience cannot be playtested.
- AI, procedural generation, or authored content owns consequences without review.

## Review Prompt

```text
What game are we making?
What does the player do?
Why does it matter?
What should the player feel and understand?
What playable proof would prove that?
What must this game not become?
```
