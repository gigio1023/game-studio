# Council Review Quality Rubric

Use this rubric to judge whether a role review is useful.

## READY

- The review names the central claim.
- The review names the proof target.
- Each active role cites checked internal evidence artifacts.
- Findings separate blockers, risks, and polish.
- Dissent is preserved instead of averaged away.
- The final verdict follows the strictest role verdict.
- Required next actions are small enough to assign.
- The next proof is specific enough to contract.

## CONCERNS

- The review reaches a usable verdict but lacks one artifact, one role
  perspective, or one bounded follow-up.
- Concerns are actionable but not yet assigned.

## NOT_READY

- The review is mostly opinion.
- Role names appear without role-specific judgment.
- Internal evidence artifacts are missing, stale, or unrelated to the claim.
- The final verdict ignores a blocker role.
- The next action is vague.
