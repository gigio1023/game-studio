# Audio Direction Review

Use this when sound effects, music, ambience, mix, voice, subtitles, or
non-audio alternatives affect play.

## Questions

- Which sound confirms the player's action?
- Which sound warns about danger or failure?
- Which sounds compete in the same moment?
- Does the mix preserve gameplay priority?
- Are captions, subtitles, or visual alternatives needed?
- Does music support pacing without hiding critical cues?
- What capture proves the audio claim?

## Blocking Concerns

- Critical state exists only in audio.
- Mix priority hides warnings, confirmation, or dialogue.
- Music tone contradicts the intended pressure.
- Subtitle or caption needs are deferred past the proof.
- Audio polish expands scope without improving play.

## Output

```text
Audio promise:
Priority cue:
Proof checked:
Internal evidence checked:
Blocking concern:
Required next proof:
```
