# Teaching And Curriculum Canon For Beginner Design Judgment: Sources Checked

[Back to index](../01-teaching-and-curriculum.md)

## Sources Checked

### External Sources

- Katie Salen Tekinbas and Eric Zimmerman, *Rules of Play: Game Design
  Fundamentals* (MIT Press): formal design vocabulary for rules, play,
  systems, emergence, interactivity, social play, and cultural meaning.
  Source: https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262240451/rules-of-play/
- Tracy Fullerton, *Game Design Workshop*, 5th edition (Routledge): exercise-led,
  non-technical, playcentric design pedagogy; covers formal, dramatic, and
  dynamic systems, emotion-focused experience goals, prototyping, playtesting,
  revision, and communication. Source:
  https://www.routledge.com/Game-Design-Workshop-A-Playcentric-Approach-to-Creating-Innovative-Games/Fullerton/p/book/9781032607009
- Jesse Schell, *The Art of Game Design: A Book of Lenses*, 3rd edition
  (Routledge): lens-based question practice across experience, player,
  mechanics, balance, interface, story, world, teams, playtesting, and purpose.
  Source:
  https://www.routledge.com/The-Art-of-Game-Design-A-Book-of-Lenses-Third-Edition/Schell/p/book/9781138632059
- Raph Koster, *A Theory of Fun for Game Design*: fun as learning, pattern
  recognition, mastery, boredom, and why games matter; official site describes
  the book as used in university-level game design programs. Source:
  https://www.theoryoffun.com/
- Steve Swink, *Game Feel: A Game Designer's Guide to Virtual Sensation*
  (Morgan Kaufmann / ScienceDirect): feel as a designed language of input,
  perception, response, indicators, metaphor, and virtual sensation. Source:
  https://www.sciencedirect.com/book/9780123743282/game-feel
- Tynan Sylvester, *Designing Games: A Guide to Engineering Experiences*
  (O'Reilly): emotion, mechanics, fiction, pacing, social interaction,
  rewards, iteration, project focus, and avoiding creative dead ends. Source:
  https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/designing-games/9781449338015/
- George Skaff Elias, Richard Garfield, and K. Robert Gutschera,
  *Characteristics of Games* (MIT Press): player-centric comparative dimensions
  such as luck, skill, effort, reward, downtime, interaction, and balance.
  Source: https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262542692/characteristics-of-games/
- Robin Hunicke, Marc LeBlanc, and Robert Zubek, "MDA: A Formal Approach to
  Game Design and Game Research": Mechanics, Dynamics, Aesthetics; designer and
  player perspectives; experience-driven rather than feature-driven design.
  Source: https://users.cs.northwestern.edu/~hunicke/MDA.pdf
- Wolfgang Walk, Daniel Gorlich, and Mark Barrett, "Design, Dynamics,
  Experience (DDE): An Advancement of the MDA Framework for Game Design," in
  *Game Dynamics* (Springer): DDE expands MDA to include broader design
  materials, narrative, audiovisual assets, player-subject, and experience.
  Source: https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-3-319-53088-8
- Anna Anthropy and Naomi Clark, *A Game Design Vocabulary* (Pearson sample
  chapter): accessible vocabulary for verbs, objects, scenes, context,
  resistance, storytelling, and design as dialogue. Source:
  https://ptgmedia.pearsoncmg.com/images/9780321886927/samplepages/0321886925.pdf
- Anna Anthropy, *Rise of the Videogame Zinesters* (Penguin Random House):
  personal authorship, DIY practice, and games as a medium for wider human
  experience. Source:
  https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/215174/rise-of-the-videogame-zinesters-by-anna-anthropy/
- Miguel Sicart, *Play Matters* (MIT Press): play as expressive, productive,
  situated human practice, not reducible to games or fun. Source:
  https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262534512/play-matters/
- Miguel Sicart, "Against Procedurality" (*Game Studies*, 2011): critique of
  procedural/rule-centered analysis that ignores player appropriation,
  interpretation, ethics, and the act of play. Source:
  https://gamestudies.org/1103/articles/sicart_ap
- Frank Lantz, *The Beauty of Games* (MIT Press): games as aesthetic forms that
  create meaning and "aesthetics of thought." Source:
  https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262048538/the-beauty-of-games/
- MIT OpenCourseWare CMS.608 Game Design readings: shows the classroom canon in
  sequence: *Rules of Play*, *Game Design Workshop*, MDA, Caillois, playtesting
  workshops, game design patterns, and postmortems. Source:
  https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/cms-608-game-design-spring-2008/pages/readings/
- MIT OCW CMS.608 instructor insights: confirms the course structure as team
  projects, commercial game play, workshops, and dedicated playtest periods.
  Source:
  https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/cms-608-game-design-spring-2014/pages/instructor-insights/
- NYU Game Center Game Design I: non-digital game creation, systems thinking,
  iterative design, playtesting, design collaboration, critical design analysis,
  and foundational texts. Source:
  https://gamecenter.nyu.edu/courses/game-design-1/
- NYU Game Center Intermediate Game Design: systems balancing, level design,
  narrative world-building, written and verbal design communication,
  iterative playtesting, critique, and collaboration. Source:
  https://gamecenter.nyu.edu/courses/intermediate-game-design/
- NYU Game Center Level Design Studio: level design as theory and practice of
  experience goals, architecture, lighting, texturing, spatial theory, and
  finished 3D spaces. Source:
  https://gamecenter.nyu.edu/courses/level-design-studio/
- Christopher W. Totten, *An Architectural Approach to Level Design*, 2nd
  edition (Routledge): spatial design, architecture, tutorial levels,
  procedural level generation, and player-focused handmade spaces. Source:
  https://www.routledge.com/Architectural-Approach-to-Level-Design-Second-edition/Totten/p/book/9780815361367
- Valve Developer Community, "Level Flow": navigability, item placement,
  advantage, attraction, player need, flow tradeoffs, and map learnability.
  Source: https://developer.valvesoftware.com/wiki/Level_Flow
- The Level Design Book, "Playtesting": level-specific playtesting,
  blind playtests, contextual feedback, and distinguishing real design problems
  from placeholder noise. Source:
  https://book.leveldesignbook.com/process/blockout/playtesting
- Nielsen Norman Group, "Usability Testing 101": participant, facilitator,
  task, realistic users, realistic tasks, observation, think-aloud, qualitative
  findings, and task success/time metrics. Source:
  https://www.nngroup.com/articles/usability-testing-101/
- *Games User Research* (Oxford Academic): game user research, playtesting,
  user testing, analytics, play experience, and HCI framing. Source:
  https://academic.oup.com/book/26677
