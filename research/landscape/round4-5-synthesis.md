Research date: 2026-05-05

Rounds 4 and 5 extend the earlier landscape research with deeper checks on

verification.

## Core Update

The strongest product position is now:

```text
Game Studio is an AI-assisted game production framework for AI-assisted game development,
with milestone contracts and stage rules.
```

This is narrower than "game generator," sharper than "production operating
system," more active than "curriculum," and more value-bearing than "agent skill
registry." Skills, curriculum, gates, schemas, examples, and docs are supporting
parts of the same promise: help humans make better game decisions under AI
abundance.

Source: [Positioning And Market Gap](round5-positioning/01-positioning-and-market-gap.md).

## What Changed

### 1. Playable Validation Is The Front Door

AI-assisted game tools increasingly optimize prompt-to-artifact speed, engine
execution, asset volume, and repo automation. The unowned gap is craft judgment:
protecting human taste, translating references into playable questions, proving
claims through small artifacts, and deciding what to cut.

Repo implication: public language should foreground "AI-assisted game production framework"
and "human creative authority." Milestone contracts should remain visible, but
they should answer what proof is being sought and what decision follows.

### 2. Beginner Pedagogy Needs Exercises, Not More Canon

The repo already has a strong craft reference index. The gap is operational teaching:
reference decomposition, formal element labs, experience chains, primary verb
feel sheets, level-as-lesson exercises, playtest interpretation, and critique
that preserves voice.

Repo implication: add small templates and worked examples rather than one large
curriculum document. A beginner should leave each interaction with a playable
question or decision artifact.

Source: [Teaching And Curriculum](round4-design-pedagogy/01-teaching-and-curriculum.md).

### 3. Player Operations Belongs Before Public Exposure

Steam Playtest, demos, Early Access, beta branches, release builds, patch notes,
community spaces, telemetry, accessibility claims, and localization promises are
different operating areas. Treating them as one "release" bucket creates
promise debt.

Repo implication: public proof should require a player operations plan. Post
launch learning is not a full production stage by default; it is an operating
ledger that can reopen proof questions when player evidence invalidates a
promise.

Source: [Player Operations](round4-liveops-community/01-player-operations.md).

### 4. Studio Lessons Are Useful Only With Context

Supercell, Riot, Valve, Naughty Dog, Supergiant, Double Fine, and Nintendo
provide useful patterns: small empowered teams, thesis clarity, kill decisions,
people-aware reboot handling, Early Access as a designed feedback system, and
the cost of extreme polish. None should be copied as slogans.

Repo implication: translate sources into lightweight contracts: prototype
question, vertical-slice production test, kill/pivot/reboot record, pride bar,
sustainability blocker, temporary cabal contract, and feedback-ready public
proof checklist.

Source: [Studio Operating Lessons](round4-studio-operations/01-studio-operating-lessons.md).

### 5. Anti-Slop Quality Must Be Operational

Quality is not polish, documentation volume, rubric confidence, or generated
artifact count. Quality is an authored, coherent, playable or inspectable
system whose experience is more specific than generic AI output from the same
genre tags.

Repo implication: add anti-slop clauses to gates and templates: generated
material is not proof, false precision is banned, human-only decisions are
logged, observed facts are separated from inferences and taste judgments, and
subtraction evidence is required.

Source: [Contrarian Quality](round4-critique/01-contrarian-quality.md).

## Source-Risk Hedges

Round 5 verification narrows several claims:

- Treat GDC 2026, Google Cloud 2025, Roblox, and Steam GenAI numbers as
  sample-aware adoption signals, not universal baselines.
- Treat Steam GenAI counts as third-party analysis of public disclosures, not
  official Valve totals.
- Treat Game Outcomes Project results as correlations, not causal proof.
- Treat Supercell and Riot as references for translated patterns, not models to
  copy wholesale.
- Treat accessibility and localization metadata as player-facing promises that
  must match shipped behavior.
- Treat AI slop as review-economy and authorship risk, not a blanket dismissal
  of every AI-assisted work.

Source: [Round 4 Claim Check](round5-verification/01-round4-claim-check.md).

## Implementation Priorities

1. Update public positioning and product boundary around milestone-gated playable validation craft
   coaching.
2. Add beginner direction templates that convert taste into playable questions.
3. Add anti-slop and provenance fields to concept, proof, review, and evidence
   areas.
4. Add player operations templates before public exposure gates.
5. Add release and live-feedback gates only where public promises exist.
6. Update skills to close with observed facts, design inferences, taste
   judgments, missing proof, next smallest proof, human-only decision, and stop
   condition.
7. Add examples that prove the routes, especially reference translation,
   auteur/systems-first, player operations, and brownfield unknown.
