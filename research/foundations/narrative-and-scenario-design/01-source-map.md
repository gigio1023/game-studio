## Source Map

### Textbook-Grade Books

- **Narrative design craft**: [The Game Narrative Toolbox, 2nd Edition](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/the-game-narrative/9781000921779/) frames narrative design as a production discipline from concept through testing.
- **Game writing fundamentals**: [Game Writing: Narrative Skills for Videogames](https://www.bloomsbury.com/us/game-writing-9781501348969/) covers dialogue, documentation, story structure, and collaboration skills.
- **Writing practice**: [Writing for Games: Theory and Practice](https://www.routledge.com/Writing-for-Games-Theory-and-Practice/Nicklin/p/book/9781032023052) is useful for vocabulary, collaboration, reflection, and workbook-style exercises.
- **Writer production realities**: [The Video Game Writer's Guide to Surviving an Industry That Hates You](https://www.routledge.com/The-Video-Game-Writers-Guide-to-Surviving-an-Industry-That-Hates-You/Dansky/p/book/9781032972619) focuses on schedule, feedback, team interfaces, credits, and role survival.
- **Storytelling for whole teams**: [Video Game Storytelling](https://www.libroworld.com/9780385345828/) targets developers who need shared narrative basics, not only writers.
- **Interactive narrative theory**: [Hamlet on the Holodeck, updated edition](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262533485/hamlet-on-the-holodeck/) remains a core theory source for agency, immersion, procedurality, and participatory story.
- **Game design vocabulary**: [Rules of Play](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262240451/rules-of-play/) gives the broader formal design language needed to keep story and mechanics connected.
- **Design questions**: [The Art of Game Design: A Book of Lenses](https://books.google.com/books/about/The_Art_of_Game_Design.html?id=69UgCAAAQBAJ) is useful as a model for review-question templates.
- **Emotion craft**: [Creating Emotion in Games](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/creating-emotion-in/1592730078/) is dated in some terminology but still useful for emotion, moral pressure, player identification, and consequence design.

### GDC Talks and Articles

- **Narrative as design**: [The Design in Narrative Design](https://www.gdcvault.com/play/1022305/The-Design-in-Narrative) argues that systems design and narrative design must be integrated.
- **Design-writing collaboration**: [Writing & Narrative Design](https://media.gdcvault.com/gdc2018/presentations/Stirpe_Eric_Writing%20and%20Narrative.pdf) is a practical GDC slide deck on shared story responsibility between writing and design.
- **Interactive story without combat or fail states**: [Interactive Story Without Challenge Mechanics: The Design of Firewatch](https://www.gdcvault.com/play/1026087/Interactive-Story-Without-Challenge-Mechanics) is useful for story-as-gameplay reviews.
- **Player inference**: [Making Her Story: Telling a Story Using the Player's Imagination](https://www.gdcvault.com/play/1023430/Making-Her-Story-Telling-a) and the [Game Developer article](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/design/video-making-i-her-story-i-and-using-the-player-s-imagination-as-a-narrative-tool) support evidence questions about what the player reconstructs.
- **Quest and level collaboration**: [Level and Quest Design Collaboration](https://media.gdcvault.com/gdc2024/Slides/GDC%2Bslide%2Bpresentations/Shen_Will_LevelandQuest.pdf) is a recent slide source for aligning quest design, level design, production, QA, and implementation.
- **Environmental story**: [What Happened Here? Environmental Storytelling](https://www.gdcvault.com/play/1012696/What-Happened-Here-Environmental), [Environmental Storytelling: Indices and the Art of Leaving Traces](https://www.gdcvault.com/play/1016815/Environmental-Storytelling-Indices-and-the), and [Environmental Narrative: Your World is Your Story](https://www.gdcvault.com/play/1012712/Environmental-Narrative-Your-World-is) support spatial narrative reviews.
- **Narrative medium fit**: [Narrative in Games: Role, Forms, Problems, and Potential](https://www.gdcvault.com/play/1018122/Narrative-in-Games-Role-Forms) is useful for avoiding film-first story assumptions.
- **Story fundamentals for games**: [Story: An Atomic Theory](https://gdcvault.com/play/1029129/Game-Narrative-Summit-Story-An) offers a vocabulary source for breaking story claims into reviewable units.
- **Immersive story mechanics**: [The Design of HoloVista: Crafting Mechanics for Immersive Storytelling](https://gdcvault.com/play/1028037/The-Design-of-HoloVista-Crafting) is useful for social-media, interface, camera, and object-mediated story.

### IGDA and Professional Resources

- **Game writing community**: [IGDA Game Writing SIG](https://igda.org/sigs/game-writing/) defines game writing broadly across dialogue, narrative structure, documentation, and other writing tasks.
- **Current SIG activity**: [game-writing.com](https://www.game-writing.com/) shows active events, posts, and community links for game writers and narrative designers.
- **Resource library**: [IGDA Resource Library](https://igda.org/resourcelibrary/) gives a broad professional index across design, AI, QA, localization, credits, and production topics.
- **Story model artifact**: [The Three Dimensions of Story](https://igda.org/resources-archive/the-three-dimensions-of-story-2020/) provides downloadable story map and key artifacts.
- **Credits and role recognition**: [IGDA Game Crediting Guidelines 10.1](https://igda.org/resources-archive/igda-game-crediting-guidelines-10-1-march-2023-update/) should inform templates when agents, writers, editors, contractors, and reviewers contribute narrative work.
- **Accessibility**: [IGDA Game Accessibility SIG](https://igda.org/sigs/game-accessibility/) and [resources for developers](https://igda-gasig.org/get-involved/sig-initiatives/resources-for-game-developers/) support narrative text, subtitle, audio, and readability review.
- **QA practice**: [Best Practices in Quality Assurance and Testing](https://igda.org/resources-archive/best-practices-in-quality-assurance-and-testing-2003/) is old but still useful as a baseline for turning content claims into testable practice.

### Interactive Storytelling and Public Craft Guidance

- **Storylets and modular structure**: [Storylets: You Want Them](https://emshort.blog/2019/11/29/storylets-you-want-them/) and [Beyond Branching](https://emshort.blog/2016/04/12/beyond-branching-quality-based-and-salience-based-narrative-structures/) explain structures that avoid branch explosion.
- **Plot and level dependency**: [Plot-shaped Level Design](https://emshort.blog/2016/05/18/plot-shaped-level-design/) connects story beats to playable sequence and dependency charts.
- **Quality-based narrative**: [Storychoices](https://wiki.failbettergames.com/) and [Design Tips](https://wiki.failbettergames.com/wiki%3Adesign-tips) are useful public references for story platforms, state, choices, and content design.
- **Production-ready branching tool**: [ink](https://www.inklestudios.com/ink/) is a mature public example of narrative scripting that supports branching, state, testing, and engine integration.
- **AI-assisted narrative research**: [Story Designer](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.09294), [Player-Driven Emergence in LLM-Driven Game Narrative](https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.17027), [PANGeA](https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.19721), and [Evaluating Quality of Gaming Narratives Co-created with AI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.04239) are useful for mixed-initiative narrative tools, LLM constraints, evaluation, and human review.


## Principles

- **Narrative design is design authority**: Treat narrative as the design of player-facing meaning through mechanics, space, dialogue, interface, audio, pacing, and consequence.
- **Writing is not a late polish pass**: Involve narrative roles before milestone scope hardens, especially when a feature changes player fantasy, motivation, choice, or consequence.
- **Scenario beats need playable proof**: A scenario claim is not ready until a player can encounter the beat, act on it, and produce evidence of comprehension or emotional response.
- **Player agency is budgeted, not assumed**: Every authored branch, state flag, fail-forward path, optional clue, and systemic reaction has production and QA cost.
- **Use structures that scale**: Prefer storylets, state-gated scenes, salience rules, hub-and-spoke layouts, and dependency charts when full branching would multiply content beyond review capacity.
- **Environmental story needs legibility tests**: Spatial evidence must answer what happened, why it matters, what the player can infer, and what action it enables.
- **Dialogue is UI and gameplay**: Dialogue carries goals, affordances, feedback, character, tone, pacing, and accessibility obligations.
- **Lore is operational data**: World facts need ownership, contradiction checks, localization readiness, and change history.
- **AI can draft, vary, and inspect; it cannot hold canon authority**: Agent outputs need deterministic constraints, provenance, review, and content locks.
- **Credits and consent are production requirements**: Narrative contributions, edits, voice work, localization, sensitivity review, and AI-assisted work need explicit attribution policy.


## Skill Integration Points

- **Game Director skill**:
  - Add narrative identity checks to direction reviews.
  - Ask whether the proposed story supports the game thesis and player fantasy.
  - Require anti-pillars for generic lore, cinematic overreach, and branch explosion.

- **Narrative Director skill**:
  - Create a first-class skill or role guide for story structure, dialogue, quest flow, worldbuilding, character arcs, tone, and content risk.
  - Require review of any milestone with dialogue, quests, authored scenes, lore, characters, barks, codex entries, or narrative AI.
  - Produce verdicts using the same `READY | CONCERNS | NOT_READY` vocabulary as other gates.

- **Systems Designer skill**:
  - Pair with Narrative Director when story depends on rules, state, progression, economy, faction systems, reputation, or AI behavior.
  - Validate that story consequences are represented in game state, not only prose.

- **Level Designer skill**:
  - Review scenario maps, clue placement, encounter pacing, critical path, optional paths, and environmental story.
  - Require spatial story beats to survive player movement, camera, lighting, and affordance tests.

- **Producer skill**:
  - Add content budget checks: word count, branch count, VO count, localization scope, QA matrix, and dependency risk.
  - Block milestones that call authored content "small" without an evidence-backed content inventory.

- **QA Lead skill**:
  - Add content QA: broken conditions, unreachable lines, repeated lines, contradiction, missing subtitles, unreadable text, wrong speaker, sequence break, and localization overflow.
  - Require playtest notes for comprehension, motivation, pacing, and emotional target.

- **Localization Lead skill**:
  - Activate earlier for narrative-heavy projects.
  - Review string IDs, speaker metadata, variables, gender/plural support, text expansion, VO constraints, and cultural context.

- **Accessibility Lead skill**:
  - Review subtitle controls, text size, reading speed, non-audio equivalents, non-text alternatives, and skip/replay behavior for narrative content.


## Role Integration Points

| Role | Narrative Responsibility | Required Evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Game Director | Approves story identity, player role, emotional target, and narrative anti-pillars. | Thesis, pillars, player experience targets, story risk notes. |
| Creative Director | Owns tone, fantasy, genre fit, and creative coherence. | Tone guide, reference board, anti-pattern list. |
| Narrative Director | Owns plot, character, dialogue, scenario flow, lore, and narrative systems. | Narrative brief, scenario map, dialogue sample, lore ledger, review verdict. |
| Systems Designer | Owns rules that create or constrain story consequence. | State model, consequence table, simulation proof. |
| Level Designer | Owns spatial delivery of scenario beats. | Beat map, critical path capture, clue/readability pass. |
| Producer | Owns content scope and production feasibility. | Content inventory, dependency list, branch budget, schedule risk. |
| QA Lead | Owns narrative verification and regression testing. | Content QA matrix, playtest report, bug list. |
| Localization Lead | Owns translation readiness and international text risk. | String table sample, context metadata, expansion test. |
| Accessibility Lead | Owns narrative access across text, audio, controls, and replay. | Subtitle/text checklist, accessibility capture, settings proof. |


## Template Integration Points

- **Direction templates**:
  - Add `Story Promise`, `Player Role in the Story`, `Emotional Arc`, `Narrative Anti-pillars`, and `Canon Authority`.
  - Require a one-paragraph story premise only after the game thesis is stable.

- **Milestone contract**:
  - Add `Narrative Hypothesis`, `Scenario Scope`, `Content Budget`, `Story Evidence`, and `Narrative Gate`.
  - Include branch count, estimated words, VO minutes, cinematic minutes, localized strings, state flags, and scenario maps.

- **Evidence contract**:
  - Add narrative evidence types: scenario playthrough capture, dialogue transcript, state transition log, branch coverage report, lore ledger diff, localization screenshot, subtitle settings proof, and playtest comprehension notes.

- **Council review**:
  - Add `Narrative Director` as an optional row that becomes required when narrative content is in scope.
  - Add `Systems Designer` and `Level Designer` rows when story depends on rules or spatial progression.

- **Work intake**:
  - Add `Narrative Inputs`, `Canon Sources`, `Forbidden Changes`, `Tone Constraints`, `Player Knowledge Before Task`, and `Player Knowledge After Task`.

- **Implementation handoff**:
  - Add `Content Files`, `State Variables`, `String IDs`, `Speaker Metadata`, `Branch Conditions`, `Fallback Lines`, and `Review Captures`.

- **Playtest report**:
  - Add comprehension prompts, emotional target checks, recall questions, confusion points, skipped text rate, and player motivation notes.
